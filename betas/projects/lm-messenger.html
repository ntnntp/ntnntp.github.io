02

Diving in

Before all else...

What does the LINE MAN Messenger user really need? Modernised user interface? More features? Promotions and discounts? Fortunately, the product management team already did a preliminary research and found that users prioritised more on better product and service above all else.

So what I need to do is make the product better, but how?

Insights, from real users

The team had interviewed an ice cream seller who primarily used GrabExpress, LINE MAN's main competitor to Messenger service for instant deliveries. In this interview, we have discovered a lot of useful insights. Here are some of them:

Price estimation is hard to acquire in Messenger
Compared to GrabExpress which can show price estimate in only few steps, Messenger requires user to follow through lengthy steps.
Promotion works; but only if service is improved
Promotions are still good incentives for users to make a switch; but that doesn't mean the promotions alone will work. Many users are still willing to pay more if it means better product and service for them.
Finding exact location is painful for user
As most map providers are not 100% accurate when it comes to address number, sellers are stuck with inconvenient process of capturing pinned location on the map, sending the screenshot to their customer, and waiting for confirmation. 
Messenger is not really suitable for business-to-business use
From the lack of order management system to difficulties in price acquisition, Messenger would not be a perfect fit for business users requiring speed and convenience.

Inspect to improve

When users launch the app, they see vibrant colours with inviting icon-based interfaces. However, when users enter the Messenger service, it looks very bland.

Moreover, Messenger is convoluted, even for simple task like getting delivery prices. As you can see below, users need to navigate through multiple steps just to select one destination and far too many steps to see the delivery cost.

We can see that instead of users being done with one location, they need to fill in the delivery info and make a confirmation too. This is like walking in an endless maze for users - when they expect their task to finish, they are given another task, and so on.

Then... what's the impact?

"Using LINE MAN Messenger is really a hassle"

— from ice-cream merchant interview

Of course. That.

Everyone loves easy-to-use products, but that wasn't the case for LINE MAN Messenger. No doubt with users choosing competitor’s service, specifically GrabExpress and Lalamove, even if it means paying a bit more.

Learn from the competitor

We have explored many messenger services including Lalamove, Skootar, Deliveroo, and GrabExpress, but GrabExpress has piqued our interest the most due to its considerably the best look and feel among others. Here are some points we have considered.

Get estimated delivery cost faster

In one of our users’ most painful point — getting estimated delivery costs, GrabExpress clearly does it better and more efficiently. If users have selected their saved location, GrabExpress will show users the estimated delivery cost immediately (given that users did not change their location). This is different approach from LINE MAN Messenger that want to make users input all the information before showing actual prices in the confirmation page.

Modernised User Interface

Look and feel of the interfaces can’t really be ignored here. LINE MAN Messenger are bland, yet overwhelming and exhausting to use because only few . This is not the case for GrabExpress which has vibrant and tidy despite being feature-rich. Also, GrabExpress uses more icons in their designs as well which can serve both as a visual aid and an aesthetic-enhancing elements.

Better Information Architecture

GrabExpress also features great use of information architecture organisation. Although GrabExpress has a lot more features than LINE MAN Messenger, its user flow and interactions are just as easy as Messenger because GrabExpress properly prioritises what most users need to do first and next.

More options and features

Speaking of features, GrabExpress has more flexible delivery options as well such as round trips, scheduled delivery, 4 hour delivery, and multiple stops. LINE MAN Messenger, on the other hand, only has round trip feature. Although many hasn’t found practical uses of following options, it is still a nice to have.

Keywords. Summarised

In short, this is what our new redesign mainly need:

Just… fast.
Fast doesn’t only mean the loading times need to be minimal. In fact, the “fast” here is actually in terms of usability. We need to take our users to their goal as fast and smooth as possible.
Smart.
To help users go through their tedious tasks such as filling in addresses faster, the application needs to help its user reduce their efforts to the minimum.
Future-proof.
The LINE MAN Messenger will surely has more features added down the road. In this major revamp, the core design needs to support both new redesign and planned features.
Complete.
As original interfaces has several things missing, specifically tracking and order history. The new redesign must include that as well.

03

Redesigning

Prioritising things

UI Redesign

So what’s the most thing in messenger “delivery” app? Of course, it’s the addresses. What this means is the fields related to addresses, specifically sender and recipient locations, should be emphasised more than any other things in relevant screens. However, in the original design, both the sender and recipient location selection button are styled just like the adjacent “Share delivery status via LINE” button.

First of all, the service should visually highlight more on the sender and recipient input by differentiating the element style, either accentuating the sender and recipient or tone down other elements. Both of the fields should convey the interconnectedness as well to better visualise the delivery process: we can either connect both sender and recipient elements with dotted lines, or merge them together into one element with two inputs.

Icons should be utilised as appropriate as well since they can be used as a visual aid indicating the function of that element and reducing user cognitive load from reading labels and texts. However, to provide more accessibility, important elements such as location selector and fields will explicitly tell users what to do in that.

Reunite with other services

UI Redesign

Let’s not forget that Messenger is the sub-service in LINE MAN app, so it would be better if Messenger update the interface designs to match with other services like LINE MAN Food or Mart in order to create solid and coherent user interfaces. Fortunately, LINE MAN already has its own improved design systems, that means what is left to be done here is to implement them appropriately. 

Spaces for marketing

UI Redesign

Last but not least, Messenger can originally show advertisements only through its Leanplum pop-up notification (the one that pops up when entering the service). We should have graphical banners on the main Messenger service interface as well to inform users about packages and promotions. These can be at the header or after crucial elements of the main interface, or even in the confirmation page as a promotion’s final call. Though, these banner should not be too distracting or intrusive. We must not forget that the priority of the redesign is to increase usability and satisfy our users as they are more interested in the better product than promotions or discounts.

Help users do things

UX Optimisation

We must go back to the fundamentals of what users will mainly do when using messenger delivery app: input addresses, add needed extra options, review order, and track the delivery progress.

Since we need Messenger to be fast and big part of users are senders, the application should select last used location by default. This means users can just pick the recipient location and be done. 

Next, users will be automatically taken to the next screen, which is more like a main screen but added elements. It will show estimated delivery cost with options to select such as round trips and vehicle options (and perhaps more if features such as extra insurance or 4-hour delivery is implemented). At this point, our users has achieved a goal of acquiring delivery prices.

Users will then need to add important delivery details in the next page as usual, or edit anything including delivery locations or selected extras. Lastly in the ordering process, Messenger will provide an overview of inputted information for users to check when users are done adding details, and if all are correct and payment methods are selected, the ordering process is done. What they have to do at this point is view rider’s location and wait. 

In the delivery tracking screen, they will be able to share delivery progress via unique URL. As this feature is the second priority of this project, it will be explained later on.

Improved location pinning

Future improvements

Well, currently pinning accurate location is... painful (pun intended)

Yes, pinning location is easy; but making it error-free is just cumbersome. To enable users more fluid experience, we will need some solutions for this challenge.

One thing I can come up with is the “get address by phone number”: since LINE MAN has quite a repository of user’s address, Messenger users can instead fill in mobile phone number and ideally be done with the address. However, an address book of each user can have lists of saved address, and in that case we will never know which one to use. Worse, when it comes to residential address, privacy is a concern. If there’s no safeguards whatsoever, this can potentially be misused.

To solve that, I’m thinking of something like a login verification. Before the phone number can be turned into an address, other ends will have a toast notification on their phone, requiring them to verify and select their saved address or pin a new one if themselves if they wish. After they have confirmed the address, the users who created an order will receive a notification that their delivery order is ready.

You might wonder what’s the solution if the other ends of Messenger user didn’t have LINE MAN app. In this case, we can have unique webpage to let these users pin their own familiar locations such as their homes or workplaces. After confirming, the rest is the same - order creator is notified and shipment begins when order creator is ready.

Also, location URLs from Apple Maps and Google Maps should be usable as well. In case that users are more comfortable working with these URLs, LINE MAN app should ideally detect the clipboard content whether it is a location URL from Google Maps or Apple Maps and show the suggestion to users in “Select delivery location” page. But sadly that is not possible (yet) because of development limitations and iOS 16 clipboard permission prompt which will come up every time when LINE MAN tries to access the clipboard just to see whether it’s an URL or not, increasing steps and can potentially frustrates user. So, we are left with simple but functional way here: users can manually paste location URLs instead of typing addresses to locate a destination.

Sharing delivery status

Future improvements

The “Share status via LINE” feature could be useful to some; but certainly not most of the users. Remember one of the insight above? This is actually another reason why Messenger is not suitable for business use. In real world situation, orders could be made anywhere outside LINE platform (Instagram, TikTok shop, Facebook pages, etc…), meaning that no LINE contact is exchanged, rendering the functionality of sharing tracking status via LINE useless.

But that doesn’t mean this function should be gone forever; actually, it should be improved to cover more of the users. For this, the solution I think will be the most practical and ideal is the sharable delivery tracking link, meaning that there will be no limitations on user platforms: user can view the status via LINE MAN app as well as browsers on mobile phones and personal computer.

However, this will be a bit problematic when (or if) LINE MAN decides to implement the multiple stops feature. What the map will show? Will users have one universal link for all recipients or one link for one recipient? Well, to protect privacy of other users when using multiple stops delivery, maps should show progress only after the rider is heading to deliver packages of that user, and the tracking link should be unique link for each user — but solution to this problem remains to be explored even more.

Results

Currently, the new LINE MAN Messenger had been in production since (date, 2022). Later phases with more features and improvements such as sharing delivery tracking or forms might be implemented in the future.

Reflections

What I have learned

In two months internship at LINE MAN Wongnai, I have learned a lot from this Messenger redesign project. Although the project is bigger than I initially thought, I’m still very thrilled to be the part of this project. Here’s what I’ve learned:

In this internship at LINE MAN Wongnai, I specifically asked for high-impact project and was assigned to redesign the whole Messenger service with added features - all of these in two months. It was challenging, but really rewarding as I have gained even more design skills throughout the task.

Find a win-win solution

One time, I have to present my designs to the marketing team, and they said they want the ad banners on top of almost everything because it was off-screen. If that happens, crucial UI elements will be pushed further down and will require scrolling to be visible, meaning increased frictions. So what I’ve done is I promptly emulated the typical screen area in the design and let them see what will be visible or not. We then seek solutions together and the way we agreed is to make half of the banner appeared and move the read-only information message down to the bottom instead. Users don’t have to scroll to reach crucial elements, the information message is still down there if user needs it, and marketing has the needed exposure. 

Being UX/UI designer means users are the priority. So a lot of times in a meeting with another department with conflicting interest, I have to immediately find an alternative solution that results in happy users while still benefits all of us. But if really necessary I still have to defend my designs and stand for my users.

Information architecture first

In confirmation page, there will be loads of information for users to verify and see: addresses, selected add-ons, payment options, banners, and more. With that many things to manage, I had an idea that sections should be ordered as ‘addresses → payment → add-ons’ because users had already chosen the add-ons and its section is for confirmation purpose only, and users won’t have to scroll to deal with payment options. However, when re-assessing the design and consulting with seniors, I discovered that the best option is still to put payment sections at the bottom even if it requires scrolling as it provide users a feel of “smoother information flow” and less confusion.

Go creative and re-iterate

Before joining this internship, I usually redesign products with user familiarity and adhering to old designs as much as possible for faster implementation; but why is it a redesign if it’s sticking to earlier designs. Currently in redesigning products, I usually list down assets and features, and start everything from scratch. I believe this way will enable me to go very creative and without constraints, just like drawing on a blank Figma project.
