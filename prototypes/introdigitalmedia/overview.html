This course will discuss an evolution of digital communication and media, and role of social norms and cultures as central components of digital
industries in relation to internet cultures. The course will explore some examples of internet and mobile phone cultures, including Facebook, e-
commerce, search engines, gaming and free and open source software production. The nature of digital media will then be analysed looking at
the following ideas: weak ties and strong ties; sub-cultures and collective identity; and the nature of labour in digital industries. The course will then
reflect on digital industries and internet cultures in relation to virtual worlds and social media.

Course Objectives

1) to familiarize students with key issues related to digital media and communication in the digital age;

2)  to enable student to analyze, given the conceptual and theoretical tools provided, the current process of social impacts that are associated with the dynamics of digital media and communication;

3)  to enable students to have a critical thinking about widely circulated social themes and practices such as digital economy and society, social networking, digital citizen and sub-culture, digitization for commercialization, employment for digital industries, globalization, and the like.
